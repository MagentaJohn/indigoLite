1) Object of the Game

Flic Flac is a timed (N seconds) turn based game. The first player to score 11 or more captures, with a lead of 2 captures, is the winner. This page summarises the rules. The following pages give more explanations and example game scenarios.

2) Movement

Each player has 6 pieces which may optionally move once during each timed turn. Successful capture(s) lead to an additional turn of shorter duration. A piece may not directly pass through, or land on another piece. There are two ways a piece can move:

  • Up to 3 coloured hexagons
  • Onto an adjacent black hexagon, which causes the piece to flip over so that the centre colours change.

A piece returns to its home position when it is captured. Additional shorter turns are for the captor(s) only. More captures leads to more additional turns.
(There is a maneuver called "indirect pass through" which helps pieces shuffle their positions. Please see example scenarios on later pages)

3) Capture

The game is played from a 2D top down view point. Each piece has a body colour (in 2D this is the outer rim) and two colours visible in the centre area. This is the top of the piece. There are two different colours hidden on the bottom of the piece.

  • A piece is said to be "flying" a colour if that colour is one of the two colours shown on the current top face.
  • A piece is said to be "empowered" if it stands on a hexagon of it's own body colour.
Pieces may only attack (and support) across adjoining borders when the flying colours match the body colour of the target piece. If one of the attacking flying colours does not match the defenders body colour the challenge is ignored for that particular border. A defending piece, under attack, flying the wrong colours, is captured as detailed below. (This is the penalty of -20) At the start of a turn each piece has a score of zero and this is adjusted, for all pieces. The following calculation occurs across each border:
  • +1 for each attacking piece flying the body colour of an adjacent piece.
  • +2 for each attacking empowered piece which is also flying the body colour of an adjacent piece.
  • -1 for a defending piece if attacker has scored +1 as above.
  • -2 for a defending piece if attacker has scored +2 as above.
  • -20 for a defending piece, not flying the correct colours so unable to defend. This heavy penalty ensures capture.
At the end of the turn, pieces with a negative score are captured and restart from their home positions. The game count of captures for both sides is updated. As usual, the piece score is reset to zero at the beginning of the next turn.
(The same scoring system applies for all pieces at the end of each turn, so losses on both sides are possible.)

4) Random Events

If configured, random events may occur at the end of any turn with a probability of 1 in 100. Such events are listed on the last page of these guidelines.
